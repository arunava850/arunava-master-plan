I've been building with AI for months. And telling almost nobody.

That changes today.

Here's who I am and what I'm doing:

20 years ago I started as an engineer. I spent two decades inside some of the most complex enterprise systems in the world — SAP S/4HANA implementations across global manufacturing, cloud architecture, data infrastructure that hundreds of people depended on daily.

I was good at it. But I always knew data was only interesting when it revealed something unexpected.

So about a year ago, quietly, I started building differently.

I used AI to automate things that were eating time. I built content systems for a real business. I started working with Cursor and AI agents to ship things faster than I ever had before. I helped build a live, revenue-generating course platform from scratch.

None of it was theoretical. All of it worked.

Now I'm doing something harder — starting from zero in public.

I'm teaching myself cricket analytics. Downloading datasets. Writing Python. Asking questions of the data nobody has published answers to yet. I'll share everything — the findings, the failures, the surprises.

I'm also building AI tools and apps and documenting every step. Not polished tutorials. Real builds, real decisions, real mistakes.

And eventually — courses, so that what I figure out doesn't stay locked in my head.

If you're curious about any of this — AI in real systems, cricket data, building things with modern tools, or just what it looks like to start something new after 20 years of doing something else — follow along.

This is day one.

#BuildingInPublic #AIBuilder #CricketAnalytics #LearningInPublic
