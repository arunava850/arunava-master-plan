# Content engine

## The core rule

**Your work IS the content.** You don't create content separately. You build, then document the build. That documentation is your content.

---

## The content multiplication rule

Every time you build something you get:
1. **LinkedIn post** — what + why + result (10 min)
2. **YouTube video** — screen recording + commentary (0 extra time if recording during build)
3. **Blog article** — deeper writeup on your website (15 min — expand from LinkedIn)
4. **Twitter/X thread** — key learnings trimmed from LinkedIn (5 min)

**One build = four pieces of content. Do this consistently and you can never run out.**

## Post formats that work

### The build post (most important)
```
[Hook — one surprising or specific thing]
[Problem that existed before]
[What I built with AI to solve it]
[What actually happened — result, surprise, or failure]
[What I'd do differently / what I learned]
[Question to reader or CTA]

#100DaysOfBuilding #BuildingInPublic #[relevant tag]
```

### The data finding post
One surprising cricket stat. Chart or screenshot. "I didn't expect this."
High shareability with cricket fans. Post on Friday.

### The "I was wrong about X" post
Contrarian or honest. Shows self-awareness. Gets shared widely.

### The week in review
Every Friday. What you built, learned, found. Compounds into a compelling story over time.
People love the weekly wrap — it makes them feel part of your journey.

### The learning post
Something you figured out. A tool you tried. A mistake you made. Teaching tone.
Wednesday post.

## YouTube guidance

- Start recording every build session now — Loom (easy) or OBS (more control)
- Don't publish until you have 5 videos ready and edited
- **First video idea:** "I built a cricket analytics tool from scratch — watch me do it"
- Cricket analytics build videos have genuine search demand — nobody owns this niche on YouTube
- Aim for 10–20 minute videos — long enough to show the full build, short enough to watch

**Recording setup checklist:**
- [ ] Loom or OBS installed and tested
- [ ] Screen resolution set to 1280x720 min
- [ ] Audio quality tested (microphone or system audio clear)
- [ ] First test recording completed and reviewed
- [ ] Backup plan if primary tool fails

## The integrated everything picture

```
BUILD something
    ↓
RECORD the screen while building (Loom/OBS)
    ↓
WRITE LinkedIn post (10 min)
    ↓
EXPAND into website blog post (15 min)
    ↓
TRIM into X thread (5 min)
    ↓
INCLUDE in Friday newsletter (5 min)
    ↓
EDIT recording into YouTube video (every 2 weeks)
    ↓
IF artist service build: post on Instagram + Facebook
    ↓
AUDIENCE grows → trusts you → buys courses → hires you → buys Dear Art services
```

Total extra time per build beyond the build itself: ~35 minutes (excluding YouTube which batches every 2 weeks)
