# Content engine

## The core rule

**Your work IS the content.** You don't create content separately. You build, then document the build. That documentation is your content.

---

## Platform strategy

| Platform | Content type | Frequency | Audience |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | AI builds, cricket, build-in-public | 3x/week | Enterprise, builders |
| X / Twitter | Short posts, cricket hot takes, AI tips | Daily or every other day | Tech + cricket community |
| Instagram | Artist tools, website builds, visual content | 3–4x/week | Artists |
| Facebook | Artist service, Meta ads, communities | 2–3x/week | Artists (older demographic) |
| YouTube | Screen recordings of builds | Every 2 weeks | All audiences |
| Website blog | Every build expanded into an article | Per build | SEO / organic search |
| Newsletter | Weekly build log | Weekly | Your most loyal audience |

**Job privacy rule:** LinkedIn = ideas and learnings only. No service promotion. Artist service lives only on Instagram, Facebook, and Meta ads.

---

## Weekly rhythm

| Day | Activity |
|---|---|
| Monday | Build day — work on current 100 days build |
| Tuesday | Write LinkedIn post from Monday's build. Cross-post to X. |
| Wednesday | Cricket or learning post — LinkedIn + X |
| Thursday | Artist content — Instagram + Facebook |
| Friday | Week in review — what I built, learned, found |
| Weekend | Bigger build or study. Record YouTube every other weekend. |

---

## The content multiplication rule

Write one LinkedIn post → then:
- X/Twitter: trim to key insight (3 min)
- Website blog: expand to full article (15 min)
- YouTube: record during the build (0 extra time if you're already recording)
- Newsletter: include in weekly roundup (5 min)

**Total extra work per platform: 3–5 minutes.**

---

## Post formats that work

### The build post
Problem → what I built → result → what I learned → question or CTA
Best performing format for builders. Use for every 100 days entry.

### The data finding post
One surprising cricket stat. Chart or screenshot. "I didn't expect this."
High shareability with cricket fans.

### The "I was wrong about X" post
Contrarian or honest. Shows self-awareness. Gets shared.

### The week in review
Every Friday. What you built, learned, found. Compounds into a compelling story over time.

### The learning post
Something you figured out. A tool you tried. A mistake you made. Teaching tone.

## Newsletter setup

- **Tool:** ConvertKit (free to 10,000 subscribers)
- **Cadence:** Weekly — every Sunday or Friday
- **Format:** "This week I built X, learned Y, found Z in the cricket data."
- **Why it matters:** Email list is the one asset you fully own. Start collecting from day one.

## YouTube guidance

- Start recording every build session now (Loom or OBS)
- Don't publish until you have 5 videos ready
- First video idea: "I built a cricket analytics tool from scratch — watch me do it"
- Cricket analytics build videos have genuine search demand
- No other creator owns the cricket + AI intersection on YouTube
