# Cricket analytics roadmap

## The approach

Starting from zero — publicly. Every step is a post. The journey is the content.

**The advantage of starting from zero:** Your audience grows with you. By the time you have something to sell, they've watched you build it and already trust you.

---

## Phase 1 — Find your data (weeks 1–2)

### Data sources

| Source | What's in it | URL |
|---|---|---|
| Cricsheet.org | Ball-by-ball data — Tests, ODIs, T20s, IPL | cricsheet.org |
| ESPNcricinfo Statsguru | Historical stats, career and match level | stats.espncricinfo.com |
| Kaggle cricket datasets | Ready-to-use IPL, T20 World Cup datasets | kaggle.com/search?q=cricket |

**Start with:** Cricsheet.org — download IPL or T20 International dataset first.

### First post when you download
> "I just downloaded my first cricket dataset. Here's what 10 years of T20 data actually looks like when you open it for the first time."

---

## Phase 2 — First analysis (weeks 3–5)

### Tools
- Python + pandas (data manipulation)
- matplotlib / plotly (visualisation)
- Jupyter notebooks (document as you go)

### Pick ONE specific question first

Don't boil the ocean. Examples:
- Which batters perform best in T20 chases under pressure?
- How does pitch type affect spinners across formats?
- Which IPL players are consistently undervalued at auction?

**Rule:** One question, answered properly, with a chart. That's a post.

### Posts this phase produces
- "3 things that surprised me in IPL data"
- "I asked one question of the data — here's what I found"
- "My first cricket data visualisation — and what it reveals"
